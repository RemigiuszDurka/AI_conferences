# Syllabus — Using AI for Didactics and Research

## Course Information

| | |
|---|---|
| **Course title** | Using AI for Didactics and Research |
| **Duration** | 5 modules (~20 hours total) |
| **Format** | Self-paced or workshop (blended learning) |
| **Target audience** | University educators, researchers, PhD students |
| **Prerequisites** | Basic computer literacy; no prior AI knowledge required |

---

## Course Description

Artificial Intelligence is transforming the landscape of higher education and scientific research. This course provides a structured, hands-on introduction to the most impactful AI tools available to academic professionals today. Participants will gain both conceptual understanding and practical skills, enabling them to integrate AI thoughtfully and responsibly into their teaching and research workflows.

The course balances technical literacy with critical reflection. We do not assume that AI is always the right answer — instead, we help participants ask the right questions: *When does AI add value? Where does it fall short? What are the risks?*

---

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, participants will be able to:

1. **Understand AI fundamentals** — Describe how large language models and generative AI systems function, including their capabilities and limitations.
2. **Apply AI in teaching** — Use AI tools to design learning materials, create assessments, personalise instruction, and provide feedback at scale.
3. **Apply AI in research** — Leverage AI for systematic literature review, research writing, data exploration, and knowledge synthesis.
4. **Think critically about AI** — Identify hallucinations, biases, and reliability issues in AI outputs and apply verification strategies.
5. **Act ethically** — Navigate institutional policies, academic integrity concerns, and broader societal implications of AI in academia.
6. **Foster AI literacy** — Design curricula and assignments that develop students' own AI literacy and critical thinking skills.

## Course Structure

### Module 01 — Introduction to AI in Didactics and Research (≈ 3 hours)

- What is AI? A conceptual overview for non-specialists
- The current AI landscape: language models, image generation, multimodal AI
- AI in higher education: global trends and examples
- Key vocabulary: prompts, tokens, hallucination, fine-tuning, RAG
- Demonstration: interacting with a large language model

### Module 02 — AI Tools for Teaching (≈ 4 hours)

- Lesson planning and curriculum design with AI
- Generating and adapting educational content
- AI-assisted formative assessment and quiz creation
- Personalised learning pathways
- Providing feedback on student work
- Using AI as a virtual teaching assistant
- Hands-on: designing a lesson plan with AI support

### Module 03 — AI Tools for Research (≈ 4 hours)

- AI-powered literature search and summarisation (Elicit, Semantic Scholar, Consensus)
- Using AI for systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- Research writing assistance: drafting, editing, and paraphrasing
- AI for data analysis: code generation, statistical support, visualisation
- Reference management with AI
- Hands-on: conducting an AI-assisted mini literature review

### Module 04 — Ethics and Critical Thinking (≈ 4 hours)

- Hallucinations and factual errors: understanding and mitigating risks
- Bias in AI systems: sources, types, and detection
- Academic integrity: what counts as AI-assisted, and what counts as plagiarism?
- Institutional and journal policies on AI
- Data privacy and confidentiality when using AI tools
- Broader societal impacts of AI in academia
- Discussion: developing a personal AI use policy

### Module 05 — Practical Applications and Workshops (≈ 5 hours)

- Workshop A: Building an AI-enhanced lecture or seminar
- Workshop B: Accelerating a real research task with AI tools
- Workshop C: Designing AI-literate assessments
- Reflection: documenting your AI use workflow
- Creating a personal action plan for AI integration
- Peer sharing and feedback

## Assessment

This course uses competency-based assessment. There are no grades; instead, participants demonstrate completion through:

| Assignment | Module | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Reflection journal | All | Short weekly reflection on AI use and learning |
| Lesson plan | 02 | An AI-assisted lesson plan for your own subject |
| Mini literature review | 03 | A 1-page summary of a research topic, produced with AI support |
| Ethics position statement | 04 | A 1-page personal statement on AI in your professional context |
| Action plan | 05 | A practical 3-month plan for integrating AI into your work |

See the [assignments directory](assignments/) for detailed instructions.

## Code of Conduct

Participants are expected to:

- Engage respectfully with peers and facilitators.
- Be transparent about AI use in submitted work.
- Use AI tools for learning, not to circumvent the learning process.
- Report concerns about AI-generated misinformation encountered during the course.
