## Key Principles Shared Across Policies

Despite variation, most institutional and publisher AI policies share these core principles:

1. **Transparency:** AI use must be disclosed clearly.
2. **No AI authorship:** AI tools cannot be listed as authors of academic work.
3. **Human responsibility:** The human author/researcher is responsible for all AI-generated content.
4. **Accuracy requirement:** AI-generated factual claims must be verified.
5. **No confidential data:** Sensitive or confidential information should not be entered into commercial AI tools.
6. **Evolving guidance:** All policies acknowledge that guidelines will change as technology evolves.

## Developing Your Own Course AI Policy

If you are developing a course-level AI policy, consider the following structure:

1. **Purpose:** What is the educational rationale for your policy?
2. **Permitted uses:** What AI assistance is allowed (e.g., brainstorming, editing, language support)?
3. **Prohibited uses:** What AI use is not permitted (e.g., generating submitted text without disclosure)?
4. **Disclosure requirements:** How and where must students declare AI use?
5. **Verification expectations:** How will students demonstrate understanding of AI-assisted content?
6. **Consequences:** What happens if students violate the policy?
7. **Review date:** When will you revisit the policy?

Sample course AI policies are available from many university teaching and learning centres. Search for `[institution name] sample course AI policy` or `AI use policy template higher education`.
