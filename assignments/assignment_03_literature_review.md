# Assignment 03 — Mini Literature Review

**Related modules:** Module 03, Module 05 (Workshop B)  
**Format:** Short literature review with AI use documentation  
**Length:** 400–600 words (review) + 150–250 words (reflection)  
**Completion:** After Module 05

---

## Purpose

Conduct a short academic literature review on a topic relevant to your teaching or research, using AI tools to support at least part of the process. This assignment demonstrates your ability to use AI for literature discovery and synthesis while maintaining scholarly standards.

---

## Requirements

### 1. Research Question

State a clear, focused research question that your literature review addresses. Example:

> *"What does the research evidence say about the effectiveness of spaced repetition for long-term knowledge retention?"*

Your question should be:
- Specific enough to be answerable with 5–8 papers
- Relevant to your field or teaching context
- Not so narrow that few papers exist

### 2. Search Process

Briefly describe how you found your papers (2–4 sentences):
- Which AI tools did you use? (e.g., Elicit, Consensus, Semantic Scholar)
- What search terms did you use?
- How many papers did you initially find? How did you narrow the selection?

### 3. Literature Review (400–600 words)

Write a coherent, synthesising review of 5–8 papers that addresses your research question. Your review should:

- Summarise key findings across the papers (do not summarise each paper separately).
- Note where papers agree and where they differ or contradict.
- Highlight at least one research gap or unanswered question.
- Be written in formal academic style.

This section may be drafted with AI assistance and edited by you, but the intellectual synthesis and any interpretive claims are yours.

### 4. References

List all papers cited in your review using APA, Vancouver, or your discipline's standard format. 

> ⚠️ **Verification requirement:** For each paper, confirm that it exists by locating it in Google Scholar, PubMed, Semantic Scholar, or another authoritative database. Papers that cannot be verified must be removed.

### 5. AI Use Documentation (150–250 words)

Describe:
- Which AI tools you used at each stage (search, summarisation, writing).
- Which prompts were most effective.
- What errors or hallucinations you encountered and how you addressed them.
- What you had to add or correct from your own knowledge.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Question clarity** | Research question is specific, focused, and answerable |
| **Synthesis quality** | Review synthesises across papers rather than summarising each separately |
| **Accuracy** | All cited papers exist and are accurately represented |
| **AI use transparency** | Clear and honest documentation of AI use and verification |
| **Writing quality** | Coherent, formal academic style |

---

## Submission Format

Submit as a single document with all sections clearly labelled. Include your full reference list at the end.
