# Assignment 01 — Reflection Journal

**Related modules:** All modules (ongoing throughout the course)  
**Format:** Short written reflections (100–200 words each)  
**Completion:** One entry per module, submitted at the end of the course

---

## Purpose

This reflection journal is a tool for consolidating your learning and tracking how your thinking about AI evolves over the course. There are no right or wrong answers — the goal is honest, thoughtful engagement with your own experience and practice.

---

## Prompts

### Entry 1 — After Module 01: Introduction

Respond to the following:

1. Before this course, what was your existing mental model of how AI works? How has it changed?
2. Which aspect of the current AI landscape surprised you most?
3. What is your primary motivation for learning about AI for teaching and research?

---

### Entry 2 — After Module 02: AI Tools for Teaching

Respond to the following:

1. Try one AI tool for a teaching task this week (even a very small one). Describe what you did, what the AI produced, and how you used or modified the output.
2. What teaching task in your context do you think AI could most improve? Why?
3. What teaching task do you think AI should never be used for? Why?

---

### Entry 3 — After Module 03: AI Tools for Research

Respond to the following:

1. Try one AI tool for a research task this week. Describe the task, the AI output, and how you verified or used it.
2. What was the most useful research application of AI you encountered in this module?
3. What would need to change (in tools, in your skills, or in institutional support) for AI to significantly accelerate your research workflow?

---

### Entry 4 — After Module 04: Ethics and Critical Thinking

Respond to the following:

1. Describe a specific situation — real or hypothetical — where using AI in your teaching or research would raise genuine ethical concerns. How would you handle it?
2. What is your institution's current policy on AI in student work? Do you think it is adequate? Why or why not?
3. Have you ever encountered AI-generated content you were not initially aware was AI-generated? What was it? How did you realise?

---

### Entry 5 — After Module 05: Practical Applications

Respond to the following:

1. Looking back at the whole course: what has changed most in how you think about AI?
2. What is the most important thing you will do differently in your teaching or research as a result of this course?
3. What questions about AI remain unanswered for you? What would you like to learn next?

---

## Submission Format

Submit all five entries in a single document. Include:
- Your name (or anonymous ID if preferred)
- Entry number and date written
- Your responses to each prompt

Total word count: approximately 500–1,000 words across all entries.
