# Assignment 04 — Ethics Position Statement

**Related module:** Module 04  
**Format:** Personal position statement  
**Length:** 400–600 words  
**Completion:** After Module 04

---

## Purpose

Articulate a thoughtful, personal position on the ethical use of AI in your professional context as an educator and/or researcher. This statement should reflect your own values, your disciplinary context, and the practical realities of your working environment.

---

## Requirements

Your position statement must address all four areas below. You do not need to follow this exact structure — you may write it as flowing paragraphs — but all areas must be covered.

### Area 1: Scope of AI Use

Describe:
- Which AI tools and use cases you consider appropriate in your teaching and/or research.
- Which uses you consider inappropriate or unacceptable, and why.
- How the boundary between appropriate and inappropriate use should be drawn in your specific context.

### Area 2: Transparency and Disclosure

Describe:
- How you will communicate your own AI use to relevant audiences (students, co-authors, journal editors, institutional committees).
- What level of detail you believe is ethically required in disclosure.
- How you expect or require students (if applicable) to disclose AI use in their work.

### Area 3: Verification and Responsibility

Describe:
- How you will ensure that AI-generated content you use or share is accurate.
- Who bears responsibility when AI-assisted work contains errors — the AI tool, the user, or both?
- What steps you will take to protect data privacy when using AI tools.

### Area 4: Broader Commitments

Reflect on at least one of the following:
- How AI use might affect equity among your students or in your research community.
- The environmental impact of AI and how (if at all) this affects your usage decisions.
- How you will continue to develop your own thinking about AI ethics as the technology evolves.

---

## Tone and Style

Write in first person. This is a professional document, but it should reflect genuine personal reflection, not institutional boilerplate. Avoid vague generalities — use specific examples from your own context where possible.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Specificity** | Statement reflects your actual professional context, not generic principles |
| **Reasoning** | Positions are explained and justified, not just stated |
| **Balance** | Acknowledges both benefits and limitations of AI |
| **Actionability** | Commitments are specific enough to guide real behaviour |
| **Reflection depth** | Engages seriously with ethical complexity rather than offering easy answers |

---

## Submission Format

Submit as a single document, clearly labelled with your name and the date. Length: 400–600 words.
