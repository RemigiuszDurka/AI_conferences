# Module 05 — Practical Applications and Workshops

**Duration:** ~5 hours  
**Prerequisites:** Modules 01–04

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

- Apply AI tools to a real teaching task from design to delivery.
- Use AI to meaningfully accelerate a real research workflow.
- Design assessments that develop students' AI literacy alongside subject knowledge.
- Document and reflect on your personal AI use workflow.
- Create a concrete action plan for integrating AI into your professional practice.

---

## Overview

This final module is built around hands-on workshops. Rather than introducing new concepts, it applies and consolidates everything from Modules 01–04.

Each workshop has a recommended duration and produces a deliverable that can be submitted as a course assignment or kept as part of your professional portfolio.

## Workshop A — Building an AI-Enhanced Lecture or Seminar (~90 minutes)

### Goal

Design a complete, AI-enhanced session for a course you currently teach or plan to teach.

### Process

**Step 1: Define the session (10 min)**

Complete the following before opening any AI tool:
- Course name and level:
- Topic of this session:
- Duration of session:
- Main learning objective (one sentence):
- Who are your students? (background, typical challenges, prior knowledge):

**Step 2: Generate a lesson plan with AI (20 min)**

Use the following prompt as a starting point, customised to your context:

```
Design a [duration]-minute [lecture / seminar / workshop] on [topic]
for [student level and background].

Main learning objective: [your objective].

Include:
- A brief warm-up or hook (5 min)
- Main content delivery with at least one active learning activity
- A formative check or closing reflection
- Suggestions for follow-up resources

Use evidence-based teaching strategies where possible.
```

**Step 3: Generate supporting materials (30 min)**

Using AI, create at least **two** of the following:
- A one-page handout summarising key concepts
- 5–10 discussion questions
- A short case study or scenario for group work
- 5 multiple-choice questions for a knowledge check
- A brief glossary of 8–10 key terms

**Step 4: Critical review (20 min)**

Review all AI-generated materials and ask:
- Is the content accurate? What would you need to verify?
- Is the difficulty level appropriate?
- Are there any biases, gaps, or cultural assumptions?
- What is missing that only your disciplinary expertise can add?

Make corrections and additions as needed.

**Step 5: Reflection (10 min)**

Write 3–5 sentences addressing:
- What AI did well in this exercise.
- What required significant human editing.
- What you would not trust AI to do in this context.

**Deliverable:** Submit your final lesson plan and supporting materials as [Assignment 02](../../assignments/assignment_02_lesson_plan.md).

## Workshop B — Accelerating a Real Research Task (~90 minutes)

### Goal

Use AI tools to make meaningful progress on a real research task you are currently working on or planning.

### Suggested tasks (choose one or combine)

**Option 1: Literature mapping**

1. Define a research question in your field.
2. Use Elicit or Consensus to find 6–10 relevant papers.
3. Extract key findings, methods, and conclusions with AI assistance.
4. Write a 300-word synthesis using AI as a drafting assistant.
5. Verify all citations.

**Option 2: Research writing sprint**

1. Take an existing outline, draft, or set of notes from your current research.
2. Use AI to expand a section, improve clarity, or write an abstract.
3. Apply multiple rounds of revision prompts.
4. Compare the final text to what you started with.

**Option 3: Data analysis acceleration**

1. Describe a dataset you are working with (or a hypothetical dataset).
2. Use AI to generate analysis code in Python or R.
3. Run the code (in a Jupyter notebook, RStudio, or Google Colab).
4. Ask AI to interpret the output and suggest next steps.

**Option 4: Reviewer response drafting**

1. Take a reviewer comment from a paper you have submitted (or a simulated one).
2. Use AI to help draft a response.
3. Revise the AI draft to reflect your actual position and findings.

### Process for any option

1. Complete the task as you would without AI — at least partially — to have a baseline.
2. Use AI assistance to accelerate or improve the task.
3. Note the time saved and quality changes.
4. Document what required verification or correction.

**Deliverable:** Submit your literature review or writing output as [Assignment 03](../../assignments/assignment_03_literature_review.md), plus a brief reflection on the AI-assisted process.

## Workshop C — Designing AI-Literate Assessments (~60 minutes)

### Goal

Redesign an existing assessment or design a new one that works meaningfully alongside AI tools — testing higher-order thinking and genuine student competence, not information recall.

### AI-resistant and AI-literate assessment strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|------------|
| **Novel application** | Ask students to apply concepts to a case or dataset they have not seen before — AI cannot predict your specific example |
| **Authentic product** | Students produce something genuinely useful (a report for a local organisation, a lesson plan for their own class) |
| **Process portfolio** | Students submit AI interaction logs with annotations explaining their decisions |
| **Oral component** | Students defend or explain their submitted work in a brief conversation |
| **Iterative reflection** | Students submit multiple drafts with reflections on changes, including AI use |
| **Peer critique** | Students critically evaluate AI-generated or AI-assisted work against disciplinary standards |
| **Personal experience** | Students integrate their own observations, data, or context that AI cannot access |

### Design exercise

Select an existing assessment in a course you teach (or design a new one).

Answer these questions:

1. What learning outcome does this assessment measure?
2. How could a student currently use AI to shortcut this assessment without demonstrating the intended learning?
3. Apply at least two strategies from the table above to redesign the assessment.
4. How will you communicate AI use expectations to students?

**Deliverable:** Your redesigned assessment brief (included in [Assignment 02](../../assignments/assignment_02_lesson_plan.md) or as a standalone portfolio item).

## Reflection: Documenting Your AI Use

A key professional skill is being able to clearly articulate how you use AI and why. This is useful for:

- Responding to questions from students, colleagues, or peer reviewers.
- Writing methods sections or acknowledgements in publications.
- Contributing to institutional policy discussions.

### Reflection exercise

Write 200–300 words in response to:

*"Describe one way you used AI in your work during this course. What did AI contribute? What did you contribute? What did you verify? How would you disclose this use to a relevant audience (students, co-authors, journal editors)?"*

## Key Takeaways

- Hands-on practice is essential for developing genuine AI fluency — reading about AI is not enough.
- The most effective AI use involves clear goals, critical review, and transparent documentation.
- Assessment design is one of the most important levers educators have in response to AI.
- A personal AI workflow — tailored to your context, discipline, and values — is more useful than a one-size-fits-all approach.
- AI integration is an ongoing process, not a one-time event. Plan to revisit and revise your approach regularly.
